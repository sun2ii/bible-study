# Matthew - The King

## Overview
Matthew presents Jesus as the promised Messiah-King, the Son of David who fulfills Old Testament prophecy.

**Author:** Matthew (Levi), tax collector turned disciple
**Audience:** Jewish believers
**Theme:** Jesus is the King of the Jews
**Key Phrase:** "This was to fulfill what was spoken by the prophet..."

## Book Structure

```
MATTHEW: Jesus the King (28 chapters)

Part 1: The King Arrives (ch. 1-4)
├─ 1: Genealogy + Birth (credentials)
├─ 2: Magi + Flight (reception)
├─ 3: Baptism (anointing)
└─ 4: Temptation + Ministry begins

Part 2: The King Teaches (ch. 5-7)
└─ Sermon on the Mount (Kingdom ethics)

Part 3: The King Acts (ch. 8-10)
├─ 8-9: Miracles (authority demonstrated)
└─ 10: Disciples sent (authority delegated)

Part 4: The King Faces Opposition (ch. 11-13)
├─ 11-12: Rejection grows
└─ 13: Parables (kingdom mysteries)

Part 5: The King Reveals Himself (ch. 14-18)
├─ 14-16: Who is Jesus? → Peter's confession
├─ 17: Transfiguration
└─ 18: Church community

Part 6: The King Goes to Jerusalem (ch. 19-25)
├─ 19-20: Journey teachings
├─ 21-23: Temple confrontation
└─ 24-25: End times discourse

Part 7: The King Conquers (ch. 26-28)
├─ 26-27: Passion narrative
└─ 28: Resurrection + Great Commission
```

## Five Major Discourses

Matthew organizes Jesus' teaching into 5 blocks (parallel to 5 books of Moses):

| Discourse | Chapters | Topic |
|-----------|----------|-------|
| 1. Sermon on the Mount | 5-7 | Kingdom ethics |
| 2. Mission Discourse | 10 | Sending the Twelve |
| 3. Parable Discourse | 13 | Kingdom mysteries |
| 4. Community Discourse | 18 | Church life |
| 5. Olivet Discourse | 24-25 | End times |

## Key Themes

- **Fulfillment** - Jesus fulfills OT prophecy (~60 references)
- **Kingdom of Heaven** - Used 32x (only in Matthew)
- **Son of David** - Royal Messianic title
- **Church** - Only Gospel to use "church" (ekklesia)
- **Righteousness** - Greater than the Pharisees
