# Genesis 1: Creation

> The structure of this chapter is the message: an orderly text about an orderly God creating an orderly world. God is not chaotic but purposeful, not arbitrary but intentional. Days 1-3 form spaces; Days 4-6 fill them. Everything builds toward humanity, then rest. Creation is a temple being prepared for God to dwell with His image-bearers.

## The Seven Days

| Day | Action | Category | Core Principle |
|-----|--------|----------|----------------|
| **Day 1** (vv. 1-5) | Light & Dark | FORMING | Separation creates time |
| **Day 2** (vv. 6-8) | Sky & Waters | FORMING | Separation creates space |
| **Day 3** (vv. 9-13) | Land, Seas, Plants | FORMING | Separation + first life |
| **Day 4** (vv. 14-19) | Sun, Moon, Stars | FILLING | Fills Day 1 (time-keepers) |
| **Day 5** (vv. 20-23) | Fish & Birds | FILLING | Fills Day 2 (sea & sky) |
| **Day 6** (vv. 24-31) | Animals & Humans | FILLING | Fills Day 3 (land) + climax |

## Conceptual Flow

```
GENESIS 1 STRUCTURE
    │
    ├─ v. 1       BEGINNING      → God creates heavens and earth
    │   └─► The absolute starting point of everything
    │
    ├─ v. 2       PROBLEM        → Formless, empty, dark
    │   └─► Not evil, but INCOMPLETE
    │       Creation needs ordering
    │
    ├─ vv. 3-13   FORMING        → Days 1-3
    │   ├─► Day 1: Light separated from dark
    │   ├─► Day 2: Waters above separated from waters below
    │   └─► Day 3: Seas separated from land + vegetation
    │       │
    │       └─► SPACES created
    │           But spaces are EMPTY
    │
    ├─ vv. 14-31  FILLING        → Days 4-6
    │   ├─► Day 4: Luminaries fill the light/dark
    │   ├─► Day 5: Fish/birds fill sea/sky
    │   └─► Day 6: Animals/humans fill land
    │       │
    │       └─► SPACES populated
    │           Creation now COMPLETE
    │
    └─ v. 31      EVALUATION     → "Very good"
        └─► Not just "good" (6x before)
            But "VERY good" with humanity in place
```

## The Opening Statement

```
THE FIRST VERSE OF THE BIBLE
    │
    └─► "In the beginning God created
         the heavens and the earth"
        │
        ├─ בְּרֵאשִׁית (bereshit) — "In beginning"
        │   └─► No article ("the")
        │       Absolute beginning
        │       Nothing before this
        │
        ├─ בָּרָא (bara) — "Created"
        │   └─► Only GOD is subject of this verb
        │       Not reshaping existing material
        │       Bringing into existence
        │       │
        │       └─► Used 3x in Genesis 1:
        │           v. 1 (heavens/earth)
        │           v. 21 (sea creatures)
        │           v. 27 (humanity — 3x!)
        │
        └─ אֱלֹהִים (Elohim) — "God"
            │
            ├─ Plural form, singular meaning
            │   └─► Not polytheism
            │       Majesty, fullness, intensity
            │
            └─► Three things established:
                │
                ├─ 1. There WAS a beginning
                │      Universe not eternal
                │
                ├─ 2. God PRECEDES it
                │      He's not part of creation
                │
                └─ 3. He CREATED it
                       By will, not accident
```

## Section Analysis

### 1. Day 1: Light (vv. 1-5)

**Key Hebrew:**
- תֹהוּ וָבֹהוּ (tohu wabohu) — "formless and void"
- רוּחַ אֱלֹהִים (ruach Elohim) — "Spirit of God"
- יְהִי אוֹר (yehi or) — "Let there be light"

```
THE INITIAL STATE (v. 2)
    │
    └─► "Now the earth was FORMLESS and EMPTY,
         DARKNESS was over the surface of the deep,
         and the SPIRIT OF GOD was hovering over the waters"
        │
        ├─ תֹהוּ (tohu) — formless, chaos, wasteland
        │
        ├─ בֹהוּ (bohu) — empty, void
        │   │
        │   └─► Together: "formless and void"
        │       │
        │       └─► Not EVIL
        │           INCOMPLETE
        │           │
        │           └─► Days 1-3 address FORMLESS
        │               Days 4-6 address EMPTY
        │
        ├─ Darkness over the deep
        │   └─► תְהוֹם (tehom) — "the deep"
        │       Primordial waters
        │       Chaos needing order
        │
        └─► Spirit of God HOVERING
            │
            ├─ מְרַחֶפֶת (merachefet) — "hovering, brooding"
            │   └─► Same word in Deut 32:11
            │       Eagle hovering over its young
            │       NURTURING presence
            │
            └─► Before creation: God is PRESENT
                Not distant
                Intimately engaged
```

```
THE FIRST COMMAND (v. 3)
    │
    └─► "And God SAID, 'Let there be LIGHT,'
         and there was light"
        │
        ├─ Creation by SPEECH
        │   │
        │   ├─ God doesn't labor
        │   ├─ God doesn't struggle
        │   └─► God SPEAKS, reality OBEYS
        │       │
        │       └─► "And God said" appears 10x in Genesis 1
        │           │
        │           └─► 10 creative words
        │               10 commandments later
        │               Words that CREATE ORDER
        │
        └─► Light BEFORE the sun
            │
            └─► The sun comes Day 4
                Light comes Day 1
                │
                └─► Light is not dependent on sun
                    God Himself is the source
                    │
                    └─► Rev 21:23: "The city does not need
                                    the sun... for the glory
                                    of God gives it light"
```

```
SEPARATION AND NAMING (vv. 4-5)
    │
    ├─ "God SAW that the light was GOOD"
    │   │
    │   └─► טוֹב (tov) — "good"
    │       │
    │       ├─ Not just "functional"
    │       └─► Aesthetically pleasing
    │           Morally right
    │           Exactly as intended
    │
    ├─ "He SEPARATED the light from the darkness"
    │   │
    │   └─► הִבְדִּיל (hivdil) — "separated, divided"
    │       │
    │       └─► Key action in Days 1-3:
    │           SEPARATION creates ORDER
    │           │
    │           ├─ Day 1: Light from dark
    │           ├─ Day 2: Waters above from below
    │           └─ Day 3: Sea from land
    │
    └─► "God CALLED the light 'day'
         and the darkness he called 'night'"
        │
        └─► NAMING = AUTHORITY
            │
            ├─ In ancient world, to name = to rule
            └─► Darkness isn't destroyed
                It's BOUNDED
                It has its PLACE
                │
                └─► Even darkness serves God's order
```

```
THE REFRAIN (v. 5b)
    │
    └─► "And there was EVENING,
         and there was MORNING—
         the FIRST day"
        │
        ├─ Evening BEFORE morning
        │   └─► Hebrew day begins at sunset
        │       Darkness → Light
        │       │
        │       └─► The pattern of redemption:
        │           From darkness TO light
        │
        └─► יוֹם אֶחָד (yom echad) — "day one"
            │
            └─► Not "first day" (ordinal)
                "Day ONE" (cardinal)
                │
                └─► Unique phrasing
                    Sets the pattern for all that follows
```

**Diagnostic:** *Do I believe God can speak order into my chaos?*

**One-line:** God speaks light into existence, separates it from darkness, and establishes the rhythm of days.

### 2. Day 2: Sky (vv. 6-8)

**Key Hebrew:**
- רָקִיעַ (raqia) — "expanse, vault, firmament"

```
THE SECOND DAY (vv. 6-8)
    │
    └─► "Let there be a VAULT between the waters
         to SEPARATE water from water"
        │
        ├─ רָקִיעַ (raqia) — "expanse"
        │   │
        │   ├─ From root "to spread out, hammer out"
        │   └─► The visible dome of the sky
        │       Separating waters above from waters below
        │
        └─► Vertical ordering:
            │
            ├─ Waters ABOVE (clouds, rain)
            ├─ EXPANSE (sky, atmosphere)
            └─ Waters BELOW (seas, rivers)
                │
                └─► SPACE for life created
                    Between the waters
```

```
THE MISSING PHRASE
    │
    └─► Day 2 is the ONLY day without:
        "And God saw that it was GOOD"
        │
        └─► Why?
            │
            ├─ Rabbinic explanation:
            │   Separation without completion
            │   Waters divided but not gathered
            │   Work CONTINUES into Day 3
            │
            └─► Day 3 has "good" TWICE
                Completing Day 2's work
                Adding Day 3's work
```

**Diagnostic:** *Am I in an "unfinished" season—separated but not yet complete?*

**One-line:** God creates the sky, dividing waters above from waters below—separation without completion.

### 3. Day 3: Land & Vegetation (vv. 9-13)

**Key Hebrew:**
- יַבָּשָׁה (yabbashah) — "dry ground"
- לְמִינוֹ (lemino) — "according to its kind"

```
FIRST ACT: LAND APPEARS (vv. 9-10)
    │
    └─► "Let the water under the sky be GATHERED
         to one place, and let DRY GROUND appear"
        │
        ├─ Waters GATHERED = Seas
        ├─ Dry ground APPEARS = Land
        │
        └─► "And God saw that it was GOOD"
            │
            └─► First "good" of Day 3
                Completes Day 2's separation work
```

```
SECOND ACT: VEGETATION (vv. 11-12)
    │
    └─► "Let the land PRODUCE vegetation:
         seed-bearing plants and trees...
         according to their various KINDS"
        │
        ├─ The land PRODUCES
        │   │
        │   └─► תַּדְשֵׁא (tadshe) — "bring forth, sprout"
        │       │
        │       └─► God empowers creation to CREATE
        │           Not just passive recipient
        │           Active participant
        │
        ├─ "Seed-bearing" — self-replicating
        │   └─► Built-in fruitfulness
        │       Life perpetuates life
        │
        └─► לְמִינוֹ (lemino) — "according to its kind"
            │
            └─► Appears 10x in Genesis 1
                │
                ├─ ORDER: Kinds are distinct
                ├─ CONTINUITY: Like produces like
                └─ DESIGN: Not random mutation
                    Purposeful categories
```

```
DOUBLE GOOD (v. 12-13)
    │
    └─► "And God saw that it was GOOD"
        │
        └─► SECOND "good" of Day 3
            │
            ├─ Day 3 is uniquely fruitful
            │   Two creative acts
            │   Two evaluations
            │
            └─► The land is now:
                ├─ Separated (from seas)
                ├─ Named (land/seas)
                └─ Productive (vegetation)
                    │
                    └─► Ready for inhabitants
```

**Diagnostic:** *Is my life producing "according to its kind"—reflecting what God planted?*

**One-line:** Land emerges, seas gather, and vegetation sprouts—the first life, designed to reproduce "according to its kind."

### 4. Day 4: Luminaries (vv. 14-19)

**Key Hebrew:**
- מְאֹרֹת (me'orot) — "lights, luminaries"
- מוֹעֲדִים (mo'adim) — "appointed times, seasons, festivals"

```
FILLING DAY 1 (vv. 14-15)
    │
    └─► "Let there be LIGHTS in the vault of the sky
         to SEPARATE the day from the night"
        │
        ├─ Day 1: Light/Dark SEPARATED
        └─► Day 4: Light-BEARERS installed
            │
            └─► The SPACES of Day 1
                Are FILLED by Day 4
```

```
THE PURPOSE OF LUMINARIES (vv. 14-15)
    │
    └─► "Let them serve as SIGNS to mark
         SACRED TIMES, and DAYS and YEARS"
        │
        ├─ אֹתֹת (otot) — "signs"
        │   └─► Navigation
        │       Omens (in ancient world)
        │       Markers of God's work
        │
        ├─ מוֹעֲדִים (mo'adim) — "appointed times"
        │   │
        │   └─► SAME WORD for Israel's festivals
        │       Passover, Tabernacles, etc.
        │       │
        │       └─► The cosmos is a CALENDAR FOR WORSHIP
        │           Not objects of worship
        │           TOOLS for worship
        │
        └─► Days and years
            └─► Time-keeping for human life
                Agriculture, history, memory
```

```
THE GREATER AND LESSER LIGHTS (vv. 16-18)
    │
    └─► "God made TWO GREAT LIGHTS—
         the GREATER light to govern the day
         and the LESSER light to govern the night.
         He also made the STARS."
        │
        ├─ NOT NAMED: "sun" and "moon"
        │   │
        │   └─► Why avoid the names?
        │       │
        │       ├─ Shemesh (sun) = Babylonian god
        │       ├─ Yareach (moon) = worshiped in ANE
        │       │
        │       └─► POLEMICAL: They're just "lights"
        │           Not gods
        │           CREATURES serving the Creator
        │
        ├─ "To GOVERN" — מֶמְשֶׁלֶת (memsheleth)
        │   └─► Delegated authority
        │       Under God's rule
        │       Regulating day/night
        │
        └─► "He also made the STARS"
            │
            └─► Almost an afterthought!
                │
                ├─ Stars = major deities in ANE
                └─► Here: brief addition
                    "Oh, and the stars too"
                    │
                    └─► De-divinizing the cosmos
                        They're just God's handiwork
```

**The Polemic:**

| ANE Belief | Genesis 1 Reality |
|------------|-------------------|
| Sun is a god (Shamash) | Just "greater light" |
| Moon is a god (Sin) | Just "lesser light" |
| Stars control destiny | God made them too |
| Cosmos = divine beings | Cosmos = God's servants |

**Diagnostic:** *Do I worship the creation or the Creator?*

**One-line:** Sun, moon, and stars are installed—not as gods, but as servants marking time for worship.

### 5. Day 5: Sea & Sky Creatures (vv. 20-23)

**Key Hebrew:**
- נֶפֶשׁ חַיָּה (nephesh chayyah) — "living creature"
- תַּנִּינִם (tanninim) — "great sea creatures"
- בָּרַךְ (barak) — "blessed"

```
FILLING DAY 2 (vv. 20-21)
    │
    └─► "Let the water TEEM with living creatures,
         and let birds FLY above the earth
         across the vault of the sky"
        │
        ├─ Day 2: Sea/Sky SEPARATED
        └─► Day 5: Sea/Sky FILLED
            │
            ├─ Waters → Fish, sea creatures
            └─ Sky → Birds
```

```
THE GREAT SEA CREATURES (v. 21)
    │
    └─► "So God CREATED the great creatures of the sea"
        │
        ├─ בָּרָא (bara) — "created"
        │   │
        │   └─► Second use of bara in Genesis 1
        │       (First was v. 1)
        │       Special creative act
        │
        └─► תַּנִּינִם (tanninim) — "great sea creatures"
            │
            ├─ Elsewhere: "sea monsters," "dragons," "serpents"
            │   (Job 7:12, Psalm 74:13, Isaiah 27:1)
            │
            └─► POLEMICAL again:
                │
                ├─ ANE myths: Sea monsters = chaos gods
                │   Tiamat (Babylon), Leviathan, Rahab
                │
                └─► Genesis: Just Day 5 creatures
                    Made by God
                    Under His authority
                    │
                    └─► The "monsters" are PETS
                        Nothing threatens God
```

```
THE FIRST BLESSING (vv. 22-23)
    │
    └─► "God BLESSED them and said,
         'Be FRUITFUL and INCREASE in number
         and FILL the water in the seas,
         and let the birds increase on the earth'"
        │
        ├─ בָּרַךְ (barak) — "blessed"
        │   │
        │   └─► FIRST BLESSING in Scripture
        │       │
        │       └─► What does blessing DO?
        │           │
        │           └─► Empowers FRUITFULNESS
        │               Enables MULTIPLICATION
        │               │
        │               └─► God's blessing = generative power
        │                   What God blesses MULTIPLIES
        │
        └─► "Be fruitful... increase... fill"
            │
            └─► Same command given to humans (v. 28)
                Sea/sky creatures: Prototype
                Humans: Climax
```

**Diagnostic:** *What has God blessed in my life that should be multiplying?*

**One-line:** God fills sea and sky with creatures, blesses them to multiply—the first blessing, empowering fruitfulness.

### 6. Day 6: Land Animals & Humanity (vv. 24-31)

**Key Hebrew:**
- צֶלֶם (tselem) — "image"
- דְּמוּת (demut) — "likeness"
- רָדָה (radah) — "rule, have dominion"
- כָּבַשׁ (kavash) — "subdue"

```
PART 1: LAND ANIMALS (vv. 24-25)
    │
    └─► "Let the land PRODUCE living creatures
         according to their KINDS"
        │
        ├─ בְּהֵמָה (behemah) — "livestock"
        ├─ רֶמֶשׂ (remes) — "creatures that move along the ground"
        └─ חַיַּת הָאָרֶץ (chayyat ha'aretz) — "wild animals"
            │
            └─► Three categories of land animals
                All "according to their kinds"
                All declared GOOD
                │
                └─► But something MORE is coming...
```

```
PART 2: THE DIVINE DELIBERATION (v. 26)
    │
    └─► "Then God said, 'Let US make mankind
         in OUR image, in OUR likeness'"
        │
        ├─ SHIFT in pattern:
        │   │
        │   ├─ Before: "Let there be..." (command)
        │   └─► Now: "Let US make..." (deliberation)
        │       │
        │       └─► God pauses
        │           God deliberates
        │           Something UNIQUE is about to happen
        │
        └─► "Let US... OUR image... OUR likeness"
            │
            └─► The Divine Plural
                │
                ├─ Option 1: Plural of majesty
                │   └─► Royal "we"
                │
                ├─ Option 2: Divine council
                │   └─► God with angelic hosts
                │       (But angels don't share God's image)
                │
                └─ Option 3: Trinitarian hint
                    └─► Father, Son, Spirit
                        In eternal communion
                        │
                        └─► John 1:1-3: "In the beginning
                                         was the Word...
                                         through him all things
                                         were made"
```

```
THE IMAGE OF GOD (v. 27)
    │
    └─► "So God CREATED mankind in his own IMAGE,
         in the IMAGE of God he created them;
         MALE and FEMALE he created them"
        │
        ├─ בָּרָא (bara) — "created"
        │   │
        │   └─► Third use in Genesis 1
        │       Used THREE TIMES in this verse alone
        │       │
        │       └─► v. 1: Creation of cosmos
        │           v. 21: Creation of sea creatures
        │           v. 27: Creation of humanity (3x!)
        │           │
        │           └─► CLIMAX of creative acts
        │
        ├─ צֶלֶם (tselem) — "image"
        │   │
        │   └─► Elsewhere: physical statue, idol
        │       Here: REPRESENTATIVE
        │       │
        │       └─► Humanity as God's "idol" in creation
        │           Not to be worshiped
        │           But representing Him
        │
        └─► דְּמוּת (demut) — "likeness"
            │
            └─► Similarity, resemblance
                │
                └─► What IS the image of God?
                    │
                    ├─ STRUCTURAL view:
                    │   Reason, morality, spirituality
                    │   Capacities that REFLECT God
                    │
                    ├─ FUNCTIONAL view:
                    │   To RULE as God's representatives
                    │   Kings put images in territory
                    │   Humanity = God's image in creation
                    │
                    └─ RELATIONAL view:
                        Capacity for relationship
                        With God and with others
                        │
                        └─► All three are likely TRUE:
                            │
                            ├─ We are LIKE God (nature)
                            ├─ We REPRESENT God (function)
                            └─ We're made FOR God (purpose)
```

```
MALE AND FEMALE (v. 27b)
    │
    └─► "MALE and FEMALE he created them"
        │
        ├─ זָכָר (zakar) — "male"
        ├─ נְקֵבָה (neqevah) — "female"
        │
        └─► Three truths:
            │
            ├─ 1. BOTH bear God's image
            │      Not male only
            │      Not female only
            │      TOGETHER imaging God
            │
            ├─ 2. Sexual differentiation is CREATED
            │      Not accident
            │      Not curse
            │      By design
            │
            └─ 3. Distinction is GOOD
                   Part of "very good" (v. 31)
                   Complementarity, not competition
```

```
THE BLESSING AND MANDATE (v. 28)
    │
    └─► "God BLESSED them and said to them,
         'Be FRUITFUL and increase in number;
         FILL the earth and SUBDUE it.
         RULE over the fish... birds... every living creature'"
        │
        ├─ Same blessing as Day 5 creatures
        │   BUT with additional mandate:
        │
        ├─ פָּרָה (parah) — "be fruitful"
        │   └─► Multiply, have children
        │
        ├─ מָלָא (mala) — "fill"
        │   └─► Spread across the earth
        │
        ├─ כָּבַשׁ (kavash) — "subdue"
        │   │
        │   └─► Strong word: conquer, bring under control
        │       │
        │       └─► Not exploitation
        │           CULTIVATION
        │           Bringing order from wildness
        │           │
```

## Personal Notes

### The First Verse Wrecks Everything

v. 1 destroys worldviews in 10 words.

```
WHAT GENESIS 1:1 ELIMINATES
    │
    ├─ ATHEISM: "In the beginning GOD"
    │   └─► There IS a God
    │
    ├─ POLYTHEISM: "God created"
    │   └─► ONE God, not many
    │
    ├─ PANTHEISM: "God created the heavens and earth"
    │   └─► God is DISTINCT from creation
    │       Not identical with it
    │
    ├─ DUALISM: "God created"
    │   └─► No eternal evil counterpart
    │       God alone is eternal
    │
    ├─ MATERIALISM: "In the beginning"
    │   └─► Matter had a BEGINNING
    │       It's not eternal
    │
    └─ NIHILISM: God saw it was "good"
        └─► The world has MEANING
            PURPOSED by a Person
```

Ten words. Entire philosophies dismantled.

### Creation by Speech

vv. 3, 6, 9, 11, 14, 20, 24, 26 — "And God SAID..."

```
THE POWER OF GOD'S WORD
    │
    └─► God SPEAKS, reality OBEYS
        │
        ├─ No struggle with chaos
        ├─ No battle with monsters
        ├─ No effort or strain
        │
        └─► Just: "Let there be..." → there was
            │
            └─► Contrast with ANE creation myths:
                │
                ├─ Babylon: Marduk fights Tiamat
                │           Makes world from her corpse
                │
                ├─ Egypt: Multiple creation stories
                │         Often involving conflict
                │
                └─► Genesis: Peaceful sovereignty
                    God speaks
                    It happens
```

This matters for me today. If God can speak galaxies into existence, He can speak into my situation.

**Psalm 33:9:** "For he spoke, and it came to be; he commanded, and it stood firm."

### The Forming-Filling Pattern

This blew my mind when I first saw it:

```
DAYS 1-3: FORMING          DAYS 4-6: FILLING
    │                           │
    ├─ Day 1: Light/Dark   ←→  Day 4: Sun/Moon/Stars
    ├─ Day 2: Sea/Sky      ←→  Day 5: Fish/Birds
    └─ Day 3: Land         ←→  Day 6: Animals/Humans
```

The structure is INTENTIONAL. God forms SPACES, then FILLS them. The world is not random—it's architecture.

```
APPLICATION
    │
    └─► Tohu (formless) → FORMED (Days 1-3)
        Bohu (empty) → FILLED (Days 4-6)
        │
        └─► God takes chaos and makes COSMOS
            Takes void and makes FULLNESS
            │
            └─► He can do the same with my life
```

### The Delegated Goodness

I used to think Genesis 1 was just about God making stuff. But look at what He EMPOWERS:

```
CREATION PARTICIPATES IN CREATING
    │
    ├─ v. 11: "Let the LAND produce vegetation"
    │   └─► Land produces, not just God alone
    │
    ├─ v. 20: "Let the WATER teem with creatures"
    │   └─► Water teems
    │
    ├─ v. 24: "Let the LAND produce living creatures"
    │   └─► Land produces again
    │
    └─► God gifts creation with GENERATIVE POWER
        │
        └─► He doesn't micromanage
            He empowers
            He blesses and things MULTIPLY
```

This is how God works: He empowers agents to act. He doesn't bypass creation—He works through it.

### The Image of God Question

v. 26-27 is one of the most important texts in Scripture.

```
WHAT DOES "IMAGE OF GOD" MEAN?
    │
    ├─ I used to think: Reason, morality, creativity
    │   └─► Things that make us "god-like"
    │
    └─► But the CONTEXT suggests: FUNCTION
        │
        └─► v. 26: "Let us make mankind in our image...
                    SO THAT THEY MAY RULE"
            │
            └─► The PURPOSE of the image
                is DOMINION
                │
                └─► Ancient kings put IMAGES (statues)
                    in territories they ruled
                    │
                    └─► The image REPRESENTED the king
                        │
                        └─► Humanity = God's IMAGE in creation
                            REPRESENTING Him
                            RULING for Him
```

This changes everything:

1. Every human bears God's image—infinite dignity
2. Our purpose is to RULE as God rules—stewardship
3. We are His REPRESENTATIVES—responsible to Him

**James 3:9:** "With the tongue we... curse human beings, who have been made in God's LIKENESS."

To curse a human is to curse God's image.

```
            Essential to fullness of image
```

### The Sabbath Setup

Genesis 1 ends on Day 6. But there are 7 days.

```
THE RHYTHM OF CREATION
    │
    ├─ Days 1-6: Work
    │   └─► God creates
    │
    └─► Day 7: Rest (Genesis 2:1-3)
        │
        └─► God rests—not from exhaustion
            But from COMPLETION
            │
            └─► The goal of creation is not WORK
                The goal is REST
                PRESENCE
                COMMUNION
                │
                └─► Hebrews 4:9-10:
                    "There remains a Sabbath-rest
                     for the people of God"
```

The whole Bible aims at this: God dwelling with His people in rest.

Genesis 1 is the foundation. Before there's a problem (Genesis 3), there's a GOOD creation. Before there's sin to deal with, there's PURPOSE to fulfill. Before humanity falls, we're crowned with dignity.

Everything else in Scripture is trying to get us back to "very good."

**Revelation 21:5:** "He who was seated on the throne said, 'I am making everything new!'"

New creation. Very good—again.
